Cell Replication

An informative website about the 2 different types of cell replication: mitosis, and meiosis. This website will show each step in both mitosis and meiosis, and having pictures along the way.


Mitosis

This is the process that somatic cells go through in order to duplicate themselves.

Interphase

Cells grow and replicate their DNA.

Prophase

Chromatin begins to form into chromosomes. Centrioles begin to form spindle fibers.

Metaphase

Chromosomes are lined up in the middle of the cell by spindle fibers.

Anaphase

Sister chromatids seperate to form different chromosomes by spindle fibers.

Telophase

Nuclear membrane splits into 2 nuclear membranes which cover each "set" of chromosomes.

Cytokinesis

Cell membrane splits apart. Chromosomes unravel to become chromatin.


Meiosis

This is the process in which gametes go through to replicate themselves.

Interphase

Cell grows and replicates it's DNA.

Prophase I

Chromatin begins to form into chromosomes. Centrioles begin to form spindle fibers. Non-sister chromosomes line up and exchange certain parts of the DNA, a process known as crossing over.

Metaphase I

Chromosomes are lined up in the middle of the cell by spindle fibers.

Anaphase I

Sister chromatids seperate to form different chromosomes by spindle fibers.

Telophase I

Nuclear membrane splits into 2 nuclear membranes which cover each "set" of chromosomes.

Cytokinesis

Cell membrane splits apart. Chromosomes unravel to become chromatin.

Prophase II

Chromatin begins to form into chromosomes. Centrioles begin to form spindle fibers. No DNA has been replicated.

Metaphase II

Chromosomes are lined up in the middle of the cell by spindle fibers.

Anaphase II

Sister chromatids seperate to form different chromosomes by spindle fibers.

Telophase II

Nuclear membrane splits into 2 nuclear membranes which cover each "set" of chromosomes.

Cytokinesis(n=2)

Cell membrane splits apart. Chromosomes unravel to become chromatin.
